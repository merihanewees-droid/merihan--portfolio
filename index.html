Clinical Education EN

Why Does Surgery Leave a Scar?

Surgery leaves a scar because the skin begins a natural healing process as soon as an incision is made. During this process, the body produces collagen to help close and strengthen the wound.

As the scar matures, it may change in color, thickness, and texture over time. If excess collagen builds up, the scar can become raised, thickened, or more visible.

Several factors can influence how a scar heals, including genetics, skin tone, wound location, and post-surgical care. Starting the right scar care at the right time can play an important role in surgical scar removal.
